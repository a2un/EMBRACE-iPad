Juan climbs into the boat.

He puts the paddle into the water and pushes the water backward.

The water moves backward, but the boat moves forward!

The force of pushing the water backward is paired with the force of the water on the paddle.

And that pushes the boat forward.

A force in one direction is always paired with an equal force in the other direction.

A pair of forces make rockets move in space.

Flames shoot out from the back of the rocket.

Then, the rocket moves forward.

Because forces are paired, for every action in one direction, there is always a reaction in the other direction.
