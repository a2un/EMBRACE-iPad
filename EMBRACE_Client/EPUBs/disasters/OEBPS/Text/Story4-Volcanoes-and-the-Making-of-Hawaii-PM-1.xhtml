Volcanoes and the Making of Hawai'i

Volcanoes spew out hot, melted rock from within the earth.

The melted rock is called lava.

Volcanoes are often found near where plates meet, but sometimes lava spews up in the middle of a plate.

The islands of Hawai'i were formed by the Pacific plate moving over a plume of lava.

The lava moves up the plume from the mantle deep in the earth.
