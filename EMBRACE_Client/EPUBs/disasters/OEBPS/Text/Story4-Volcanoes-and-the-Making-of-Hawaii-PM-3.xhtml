After the plate moves, the hotspot spews up lava to form a new island.

This is the island of Oahu that was made about three million years ago.

The Pacific plate kept moving and made all of the other Hawaiian Islands.

The hotspot is still there, and it is adding rock to the Big Island (also called Hawai’i).

Visitors to Hawai’i can see the lava coming to the top and making the island bigger.

But the lava can destroy buildings and roads, and hot gases from volcanoes can kill people.
